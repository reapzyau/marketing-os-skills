# Post Frameworks

9 frameworks distilled from Matt Gray's Content Empire OS and Justin Welsh's Content Operating System. Each framework includes LinkedIn-specific formatting guidance.

---

## 1. Educational List

**Best for:** Tips, numbered advice, step-by-step how-tos
**Origin:** Both (Gray's "Helpful Content" quadrant + Welsh's "Do These X Things")
**Emotion:** Educate

**When to use:** You have 3-7 actionable items around one topic. The audience can implement immediately.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook — what they'll learn or why it matters]

[1-2 sentences of context or setup]

1. [Item + brief explanation]
2. [Item + brief explanation]
3. [Item + brief explanation]
4. [Item + brief explanation]
5. [Item + brief explanation]

[Takeaway line — tie it together]
[Soft CTA or engagement prompt]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
[Number] [things/habits/rules/steps] to [desired outcome]:

1. [Thing] — [why it matters in <10 words]
2. [Thing] — [why it matters]
3. [Thing] — [why it matters]
...

[Formula: X + Y = Z outcome]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Can expand each item to 2-3 lines. 800-1300 chars total.

## 2. Story + Lesson

**Best for:** Personal narrative with a takeaway, experience-based authority
**Origin:** Gray (REV principle — Real, Engaging, Vulnerable + "Reflection" quadrant)
**Emotion:** Empathize / Educate

**When to use:** You have a personal experience (win, failure, surprise, turning point) that teaches something your audience needs to hear.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook — set the scene or state the surprising outcome]

[The situation — what happened, 2-3 sentences]

[The turning point — what changed, what you realized]

[The lesson — what you learned, stated clearly]

[Application — how the reader can use this]
[Soft CTA or engagement prompt]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
[Time reference] ago, I [situation that sounds relatable].

[What I was doing / thinking / believing]

Then [the thing that changed everything].

[What I realized / learned]

The lesson: [one clear sentence]

[Ask audience about their similar experience]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Full narrative. Use line breaks between every 1-2 sentences for readability.

## 3. Old Way vs New Way

**Best for:** Contrarian reframing, showing evolution, challenging conventional wisdom
**Origin:** Welsh (curated from Mark Tenenholtz)
**Emotion:** Challenge / Educate

**When to use:** You want to contrast outdated approaches with modern ones. Works especially well when the "old way" is what most of your audience currently does.

**Structure:**
```
How to [do thing]:

Old way:
- [Outdated approach 1]
- [Outdated approach 2]
- [Outdated approach 3]
- [Outdated approach 4]

New way:
- [Modern approach 1]
- [Modern approach 2]
- [Modern approach 3]
- [Modern approach 4]

[Takeaway — why the shift matters]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
How to [outcome] in [year/era]:

Old way:
- [Thing everyone still does]
- [Thing everyone still does]
- [Thing everyone still does]

New way:
- [What actually works now]
- [What actually works now]
- [What actually works now]

[One-line principle: "Trust over gimmicks" / "Systems over hustle"]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Add a paragraph after the lists explaining WHY the shift happened.

## 4. Two Types of People

**Best for:** Identity contrast, making audience self-select, creating engagement through recognition
**Origin:** Welsh (spotted on Amanda Natividad's profile)
**Emotion:** Challenge

**When to use:** You want to draw a clear line between two mindsets or behaviors. The audience should immediately recognize which type they are — and want to be the better one.

**Structure:**
```
There are two types of [people/founders/marketers]:

1. [Type A — the common/losing approach]
   [1-2 sentences describing behavior]

2. [Type B — the winning approach]
   [1-2 sentences describing behavior]

[Implication — which one are you?]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
There are two types of [identity]:

Type 1: [Does X, believes Y, gets Z result]
Type 2: [Does A, believes B, gets C result]

The difference? [One insight that separates them]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Expand each type to a full paragraph. Add a question at the end.

## 5. Biggest Mistake

**Best for:** Vulnerability + authority, curiosity-driven hooks, showing growth
**Origin:** Welsh (curated from Nicholas Cole)
**Emotion:** Empathize / Educate

**When to use:** You have a genuine mistake or regret that taught you something valuable. The hook creates instant curiosity.

**Structure:**
```
The biggest mistake I made as a [identity/role]?

[State the mistake — one clear sentence]

[Add context — why it happened, 2-3 sentences]

Instead, [action verb — do/try/build/read]:

1. [What to do instead]
2. [What to do instead]
3. [What to do instead]
4. [What to do instead]
5. [What to do instead]

[The winning outcome — what changed when you fixed it]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
The biggest mistake I made as a [role]?

[Mistake in one sentence].

[Why I made it / what I was thinking]

Here's what I do now:

1. [Action]
2. [Action]
3. [Action]

[Result since making the change]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Full version with expanded context. 800-1300 chars.

## 6. Framework Share

**Best for:** System/process reveals, intellectual property display, "here's my playbook" posts
**Origin:** Gray (Content OS, Founder Flywheel — sharing actual frameworks)
**Emotion:** Educate

**When to use:** You have a repeatable process, system, or mental model. You're giving away the playbook.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook — name the framework or promise the outcome]

Here's my [name] framework:

Step 1: [Action]
[1-2 sentences explaining]

Step 2: [Action]
[1-2 sentences explaining]

Step 3: [Action]
[1-2 sentences explaining]

[Result — what happens when you follow this]
[Soft CTA]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
I use a [number]-step [framework/system/process] for [outcome]:

Step 1: [Verb] — [what and why]
Step 2: [Verb] — [what and why]
Step 3: [Verb] — [what and why]

This is how I [specific result with numbers if possible].

[Save this for later / Which step do you struggle with?]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Full expansion. Each step gets 2-3 lines. Name the framework.

## 7. Hot Take

**Best for:** Bold claims, debate starters, pattern interrupts, going against the grain
**Origin:** Gray (Copywriting OS — "Challenge" emotion + Vulnerable Content quadrant)
**Emotion:** Challenge / Entertain

**When to use:** You have a genuine opinion that contradicts mainstream advice. Must be defensible — not contrarian for clicks.

**Structure:**
```
[Bold statement — the hot take itself]

[Why most people believe the opposite]

[Your evidence — experience, data, or logic]

[The reframe — what they should believe instead]

[Engagement prompt — agree or disagree?]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
[Unpopular opinion / Hot take / Controversial but true]:

[The claim in one sentence]

Everyone says [conventional wisdom].

But here's what I've seen: [your counter-evidence]

The truth: [reframe]

[Am I wrong? / Fight me in the comments / What's your take?]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Expand the evidence section. Include a specific story or data point.

## 8. Do These X Things

**Best for:** Actionable listicles, directive authority content, "if you're X, do Y" posts
**Origin:** Welsh (curated from Dan Coe)
**Emotion:** Educate

**When to use:** You want to give direct, authoritative advice to a specific audience segment. The "if you are X" opener qualifies the reader immediately.

**Structure:**
```
If you are [identity/situation]:

Stop.

You must [do/have/know] these [number] things:

1. [Thing + brief context]
2. [Thing + brief context]
3. [Thing + brief context]

[Formula: X + Y = Z outcome]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
If you're [specific person doing specific thing]:

[Stop / Listen / Pay attention]:

[Number] things that will [change/fix/accelerate] [outcome]:

1. [Thing]
2. [Thing]
3. [Thing]

[Positive formula or promise]
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Expand each item. Add context after the list.

## 9. Research/Data Share

**Best for:** Original analysis, data-led thought leadership, establishing authority through proprietary insights
**Origin:** Competitor analysis of Kevin Indig's LinkedIn (March 2026) — this pattern dominated his top-performing posts
**Emotion:** Educate / Challenge

**When to use:** You have data, an audit, a study, or an analysis with specific numbers. You're sharing findings that your audience can't get elsewhere. This is the highest-performing framework for enterprise-targeting B2B thought leadership.

**Structure:**
```
[Hook — "I analysed [number] [things] to [understand/find] [insight]."]

[Setup — why this matters, what question you were answering, 1-2 sentences]

[Key findings — 2-4 numbered data points with specific numbers]

[Implication — what this means for the reader's strategy/approach]

[Reframe — the contrarian insight or "so what"]

[CTA — "Full analysis in comments" or engagement prompt]
```

**Skeleton (fill-in):**
```
I [analysed/audited/tracked/studied] [specific number] [data points] to [understand/find] [question].

[1-2 sentences on why this matters or what prompted the research]

The data reveals:
1. [Finding with specific number] — [implication]
2. [Finding with specific number] — [implication]
3. [Finding with specific number] — [implication]

[What this means for your audience — one clear sentence]

[Contrarian reframe or "so what" insight]

Full analysis in the comments.
```

**LinkedIn notes:** Always pair with a chart, data visualisation, or screenshot image. Image posts in this framework average ~2x the engagement of text-only. Medium length (500-1500 chars) is the sweet spot.

**Key differentiator:** The specific number in the hook is non-negotiable. "1.2 million search results" beats "a lot of search results." "11,022 citations" beats "thousands of citations." The number IS the credibility.

## Framework Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Framework |
|-----------|---------------|
| You have data or original research | Research/Data Share (highest performer) |
| You have actionable tips | Educational List or Do These X Things |
| You have a personal story | Story + Lesson or Biggest Mistake |
| You want to challenge thinking | Old Way vs New Way or Hot Take |
| You want to reveal your process | Framework Share |
| You want engagement/debate | Two Types of People or Hot Take |
| You want to show vulnerability | Biggest Mistake or Story + Lesson |
| You want saves/bookmarks | Educational List or Framework Share |
| You want comments | Two Types of People or Hot Take or Research/Data Share |
