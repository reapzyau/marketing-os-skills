# Scoring Matrix Reference

Detailed calibration guide for the 8 scoring dimensions used in Step 3 of the YouTube Topic Ideator.

---

## 1. TAM Resonance (20% weight)

**What it measures:** How large is the potential audience for this topic?

**Data sources:** YouTube search result view counts + Google Trends interest level

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | YouTube Signal | Trends Signal |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| 9-10 | Top results have 500K+ views; many videos on topic | Interest score 70-100; broad search term |
| 7-8 | Top results have 100K-500K views; moderate competition | Interest score 40-69; niche but active |
| 5-6 | Top results have 20K-100K views; sparse coverage | Interest score 20-39; smaller audience |
| 3-4 | Top results under 20K views; very few videos | Interest score 10-19; micro-niche |
| 1-2 | Almost no videos; negligible views | Interest score under 10; no measurable demand |

### Interpretation Notes

- High TAM alone doesn't mean you should make the video -- it must be paired with a Competition Gap
- A Core Ring topic with moderate TAM (5-6) often outperforms an Outer Ring topic with high TAM (9-10) because conversion is higher
- Adjust expectations by niche: 50K views in "knitting" is a mega-hit; 50K in "gaming" is average

## 2. Shock Score (15% weight)

**What it measures:** How surprising or counterintuitive is the core insight/fact of this video?

**Scored collaboratively with user.** Ask: "On a scale of 1-100, if you told 100 random people this fact, how many would be genuinely surprised?"

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | Shock Level | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 90-100 | Mind-blowing -- challenges fundamental assumptions | "Most gym owners make more from their parking lot than memberships" |
| 80-89 | Genuinely surprising -- makes people say "wait, really?" | "The #1 reason members cancel isn't price -- it's they can't find parking" |
| 60-79 | Interesting -- new information but not shocking | "Gyms with a referral program retain 23% more members" |
| 40-59 | Somewhat notable -- confirms a suspicion with data | "Most gym members only come 4.7 times per month" |
| 20-39 | Common knowledge dressed up differently | "Consistency is the key to fitness results" |
| 1-19 | Obvious -- no one would be surprised | "Working out is good for you" |

### The 80+ Rule

Only build the video around facts scoring 80+. If nothing scores 80+:
- Change the angle (same topic, different frame)
- Add a surprising constraint ("...in just 30 days", "...with $0 budget")
- Find the contrarian take ("Why [common advice] is actually wrong")
- Stack two moderately surprising facts into one compound revelation

### Common Pitfalls

- **Creator bias**: You've been in your niche so long that nothing surprises you anymore. Score from the AUDIENCE's perspective, not yours.
- **Specificity amplifies shock**: "Gyms lose money" (score: 40) vs "The average gym loses $127/member/year on classes nobody attends" (score: 75)
- **Framing matters**: The same fact can score 50 or 85 depending on how you frame it

## 3. Content Ring Fit (15% weight)

**What it measures:** How closely does this topic align with your channel's core subject matter?

**Based on the 40-40-20 rule** from Kallaway's framework.

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | Ring | Description | Reach/Conversion Tradeoff |
|-------|------|-------------|--------------------------|
| 10 | Core (40%) | Directly about your niche topic | Lowest reach, highest conversion |
| 7 | Inner (40%) | Tangential -- your audience cares about this | Balanced reach and conversion |
| 4 | Outer (20%) | Broad appeal with a niche angle | Highest reach, lowest conversion |

### Decision Tree

```
Is this topic DIRECTLY about your core subject?
├── YES → Core Ring (10)
└── NO → Would your existing audience actively seek this out?
    ├── YES → Inner Ring (7)
    └── NO → Can you add a niche angle that makes it relevant?
        ├── YES → Outer Ring (4)
        └── NO → Skip this topic (doesn't fit your channel)
```

### Content Ring Balance

A healthy channel follows the 40-40-20 distribution over time:
- **40% Core**: Builds authority, converts subscribers
- **40% Inner**: Retains audience, shows range
- **20% Outer**: Attracts new viewers, viral potential

If your recent videos are all Core, prioritize Inner/Outer ideas. If you've been chasing views with Outer content, return to Core for authority.

### The Niche Hook Rule

Outer Ring topics MUST have a niche hook -- the specific angle that makes it relevant to your channel. Without it, the video attracts viewers who'll never subscribe.

Example for a boxing fitness channel:
- "How to Build Discipline" = generic (no niche hook)
- "How Boxers Build Unbreakable Discipline" = Outer with niche hook

## 4. Competition Gap (15% weight)

**What it measures:** How much opportunity exists relative to existing content on this topic?

**Data source:** YouTube search results (count, quality, age, view distribution)

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | Classification | Signal |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| 9-10 | Blue Ocean | 0-5 quality videos; existing ones are old (>1yr) or amateur; high views on the few that exist |
| 7-8 | Green Field | 5-15 videos; mixed quality; room for a standout entry |
| 5-6 | Moderate | 15-25 videos; some professional content; need a differentiated angle |
| 3-4 | Red Ocean | 25+ videos; professional production; established creators dominating |
| 1-2 | Dead Zone | Many videos AND low views across the board = no demand |

### How to Read YouTube Search Results

1. **Count videos**: Search the topic, count results on first 2 pages with >1K views
2. **Check dates**: Are the top results from the last 6 months? Or 2+ years old?
3. **Assess quality**: Professional thumbnails? Clear titles? Good production?
4. **View distribution**: Do views cluster at the top (winner-take-all) or spread evenly?

### Red Ocean Isn't Always Bad

A Red Ocean topic with a strong **angle differentiator** can still work:
- You have unique data/experience nobody else has
- You can add Shock Score 80+ the existing videos lack
- Your production quality or personality is clearly superior
- You're approaching from a Content Ring nobody's used (e.g., everyone teaches it -- you investigate it)

### Dead Zone Warning

Dead Zone (many videos, all low views) usually means the topic lacks genuine audience demand. Avoid unless:
- You have evidence the topic is newly emerging (Google Trends growing)
- Existing videos all have terrible titles/thumbnails (packaging failure, not demand failure)

## 5. Creator Passion (10% weight)

**What it measures:** How excited is the creator about making this specific video?

**Scored by the user directly.** Ask: "Rate your excitement for making this video, 1-10."

### Why This Matters (MrBeast Gate)

MrBeast won't greenlight a video his team isn't genuinely excited about -- even if the data says it'll perform. Reason: excitement translates to better delivery, more creative effort, and willingness to reshoot until it's right.

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 9-10 | "I've been thinking about this topic for weeks, I NEED to make this video" |
| 7-8 | "This is interesting and I'd enjoy making it" |
| 5-6 | "I can see why this would work but I'm not particularly excited" |
| 3-4 | "I'd make this if the data was strong enough, but I wouldn't choose it" |
| 1-2 | "I'd dread making this video" |

### The Passion Floor

Ideas scoring below 5 on Creator Passion should be flagged with a warning. A video you're not excited about will have flat energy, fewer retakes, and a script that feels like obligation rather than enthusiasm. The audience feels this.

### Exception: Data Override

If an idea scores 9+ on TAM, 9+ on Competition Gap, and 80+ Shock Score, it may be worth making even at passion 5-6. But set a hard floor at 4 -- never make a video you actively don't want to make.

## 6. Outlier Proof (10% weight)

**What it measures:** Is there evidence from existing outlier videos that this topic/angle combination works?

**Data source:** Outlier mining results from Step 1A

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | Evidence Level |
|-------|---------------|
| 9-10 | Direct outlier: a video on this exact topic got 5x+ channel average views |
| 7-8 | Strong analog: a very similar topic/angle was an outlier on a comparable channel |
| 5-6 | Weak analog: the topic worked but with a different angle, or the angle worked with a different topic |
| 3-4 | Indirect signal: related topics perform well but no direct match |
| 1-2 | No outlier data available; untested territory |

### Interpreting Outlier Data

- **5x+ channel average** = strong outlier signal
- **3-5x channel average** = moderate signal (might be packaging, not topic)
- **1-3x channel average** = normal performance (not an outlier)

### When Low Outlier Proof Is OK

A score of 1-3 isn't a dealbreaker if:
- The topic is genuinely novel (Blue Ocean -- nobody's tried it yet)
- Google Trends shows growing interest (emerging demand)
- Reddit/comment mining shows strong audience desire
- The Shock Score is 85+ (the insight itself is the differentiator)

## 7. Trend Direction (10% weight)

**What it measures:** Is interest in this topic growing, stable, or declining?

**Data source:** Google Trends 12-month data

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | Trend Shape | Interpretation |
|-------|------------|----------------|
| 9-10 | Strongly growing | Interest doubled or more over 12 months -- ride the wave |
| 7-8 | Moderately growing | Steady upward trend -- good timing |
| 5-6 | Stable | Flat line -- evergreen topic, reliable but no timing advantage |
| 4-5 | Seasonal | Predictable spikes -- time the video to hit 2-4 weeks before peak |
| 2-3 | Declining | Falling interest -- still viable if angle is strong, but headwind |
| 1 | Collapsed | Interest dropped 50%+ -- avoid unless contrarian "comeback" angle |

### How to Read Google Trends Data

**Common traps:**
- **Noise vs signal**: Small fluctuations week-to-week are noise. Look at the 3-month moving average.
- **Seasonal patterns**: Some topics spike annually (New Year's fitness, tax season). This isn't "growing" -- it's seasonal. Check if the BASELINE between spikes is rising.
- **Relative vs absolute**: Google Trends shows relative interest (0-100 scale). A topic at "30" with 50M monthly searches is bigger than a topic at "80" with 100K searches. Cross-reference with YouTube search volume.
- **Geographic relevance**: Default is worldwide. Filter to your target geography (usually US, UK, or specific country).

### Seasonal Timing Strategy

If a topic has seasonal patterns:
- Publish **2-4 weeks before** the peak (YouTube's algorithm needs time to pick up the video)
- Seasonal topics at their trough score 2-3; at their peak approach score 7-8
- Score based on CURRENT timing relative to the cycle

## 8. Evergreen Potential (5% weight)

**What it measures:** Will this topic still be relevant and searchable 6+ months from now?

### Scoring Calibration

| Score | Shelf Life | Examples |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| 9-10 | Years -- fundamentally evergreen | "How to [fundamental skill]", "Why [timeless principle]" |
| 7-8 | 1-2 years -- durable with minor updates | "Best [tools] for [use case]", "How to start [business type]" |
| 5-6 | 6-12 months -- current but not urgent | "2026 trends in [niche]", "[Current method] explained" |
| 3-4 | 1-6 months -- timely but fading | News reactions, product launches, algorithm updates |
| 1-2 | Days to weeks -- pure news/reaction | Celebrity drama, breaking news, meme-of-the-week |

### The Evergreen-Viral Tradeoff

- Evergreen topics compound over time (search traffic grows)
- Topical topics spike and die (algorithm push, then nothing)
- Best case: a topical ANGLE on an evergreen TOPIC (scores 7-8)
- Example: "How Gym Owners Should Respond to the 2026 Economy" = evergreen topic (gym business) + timely angle (2026 economy)

### Low Weight, High Signal

Evergreen Potential is only 5% weight because some channels thrive on topical content. But for most creators, evergreen topics are the foundation that keeps generating views long after upload.

## Composite Scoring Example

**Topic: "Why Most Gym Owners Are Accidentally Breaking the Law"**

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| TAM Resonance | 7 | "gym owner" searches moderate; legal content gets curiosity clicks |
| Shock Score | 85 | Most gym owners don't know about specific compliance issues |
| Content Ring | Core (10) | Directly about gym business |
| Competition Gap | 8 | Few quality videos on gym compliance; mostly boring legal content |
| Creator Passion | 7 | User finds the topic interesting but not their favorite |
| Outlier Proof | 6 | Similar "you're doing it wrong" angles work in business niches |
| Trend Direction | 6 | Stable interest in gym business; no strong trend |
| Evergreen Potential | 8 | Compliance requirements don't change fast |

**Ideation Score:**
(7×0.20) + (8.5×0.15) + (10×0.15) + (8×0.15) + (7×0.10) + (6×0.10) + (6×0.10) + (8×0.05)
= 1.40 + 1.275 + 1.50 + 1.20 + 0.70 + 0.60 + 0.60 + 0.40
= **7.675 → 76.8/100**

Verdict: Strong candidate. High Shock Score and Blue Ocean competition make this worth developing despite moderate TAM.
