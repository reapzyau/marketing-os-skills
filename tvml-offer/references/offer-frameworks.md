# TVML Offer Framework Reference

The Vibe Marketing Lab's proprietary offer-building framework.

---

## 1. Value Design

### The Value Equation

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

- **Dream Outcome** — What they want (increase this)
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — How likely they believe they'll get it (increase this)
- **Time Delay** — How long it takes to see results (decrease this)
- **Effort & Sacrifice** — What they have to give up or do (decrease this)

The goal: make an offer so good people feel stupid saying no.

Rate each factor 1-10 and calculate the score for every component in the value stack.

### Factors of Value

For EVERY component in your offer, pre-empt every concern by answering:

1. **What is it?** — Plain language description
2. **Why do they need it?** — The problem it solves
3. **What happens if they DON'T have it?** — Pain of inaction
4. **What happens when they DO have it?** — Pleasure of action
5. **How does it work?** — Brief method/process
6. **Why is YOUR version better?** — Differentiator
7. **What proof do you have?** — Evidence it works

This forces you to build a value argument for every piece of the offer — not just the headline.

### Pricing Psychology

- **Price on value, not cost.** Your price reflects the OUTCOME, not the hours.
- **Charge more, deliver more.** Higher prices fund better results, which create better testimonials, which create more demand.
- **The Virtuous Cycle:** Higher price → better clients → more attention → better results → better proof → higher price
- **Value-to-price ratio:** Aim for perceived value of 10x+ the price. If you charge $1,000, the perceived value should be $10,000+.
- **Never compete on price.** Compete on value. If you're the cheapest, you're commoditized.

### 80/20 Pricing Fractal

- 20% of buyers will pay 5x the base price
- 4% of buyers will pay 25x the base price
- Always consider tier pricing: Base → 5x Premium → 25x Ultra
- Reverse-engineer willingness to pay from avatar income and pain level

### Prepayment & Billing Structures

- **Guarantee + Prepay:** Give the guarantee only if they prepay. No prepay, no guarantee.
- **Layaway:** Start paying now, start delivering when they've paid half/full.
- **BNPL:** Third-party financing (Affirm, Klarna). Typically ~35% sales boost.
- **Weekly vs monthly billing:** Weekly collects more reliably for lower-ticket recurring offers.

### 4 Ways to Display the Same Discount

| Method | Example | Best When |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Percentage off | "87% off first visit" | Amount under $100 |
| Absolute amount off | "$181 off first week (normally $210)" | Amount over $100 |
| Relative equivalent | "Save a steak dinner" | Emotional resonance needed |
| Just the discounted price | "$29 New Client Special" | Service is well-understood |

**Rule of thumb:** Percentage for amounts under $100. Dollar amount for amounts over $100.

## 2. Offer Architecture

### 5-Step Offer Build

**Step 1: Identify the Dream Outcome**
- What does the customer ACTUALLY want?
- Express it as specifically and viscerally as possible
- Not "lose weight" — "Drop 20lbs in 12 weeks so you look amazing at your reunion"

**Step 2: List ALL the Problems**
Map every problem in three timeframes: Before, During, and After (see Section 3).

**Step 3: Create Solutions for Every Problem**
- For EVERY problem, design a specific solution
- Each unsolved problem is an objection that kills the sale
- Solutions can be: content, tools, templates, access, community, coaching, done-for-you

**Step 4: Choose Delivery Vehicles**

| Delivery Type | Effort Level | Perceived Value |
|---|---|---|
| 1-on-1 coaching/consulting | High (you) | Highest |
| Small group (cohort) | Medium | High |
| 1-to-many (course/content) | Low | Medium |
| Done-for-you (DFY) | High (you) | Highest |
| Done-with-you (DWY) | Medium | High |
| Do-it-yourself (DIY) | Low | Lower |

**Principle:** Combine high-value perception with sustainable delivery.

**Step 5: Trim & Stack**
- Remove low-value, high-cost solutions
- Keep high-value, low-cost solutions (things that cost you little but save them a lot)
- Stack remaining solutions into a cohesive offer
- Name each component (named = higher perceived value)
- Assign a perceived dollar value to each component

### Offer Type Classification

**Consultation Offer:**
- For complex/expensive purchases where the buyer needs guidance
- The CTA is a conversation, not a purchase
- You're selling the NEXT STEP, not the whole thing
- Examples: Custom builds, agency services, enterprise software

**Ready-Now Offer:**
- For straightforward purchases where the buyer knows what they want
- The CTA is a direct purchase or sign-up
- You need to close the deal in the offer itself
- Examples: Gym memberships, online courses, productized services

**Key insight:** Most businesses use the wrong type. Match the offer type to the purchase complexity.

### 4-Component Framework

1. **The Headline Offer** — One sentence capturing the COMPLETE value proposition. Format: "[Result] for [Avatar] in [Timeframe] — [Method/Differentiator]"
2. **The Benefit Bullets** — 5-7 formatted bullets (see Section 6)
3. **The Guarantee** — Risk reversal matched to offer type (see Section 5)
4. **The Tangible Bonus / Ethical Bribe** — Something valuable for taking the next step (NOT a discount)

### Delivery Model Principle

- If the product is EASY to deliver (templates, courses, automated), ACQUISITION must be hard (marketing, content, authority, long nurture)
- If the product is HARD to deliver (custom, high-touch, DFY), ACQUISITION can be easy (referrals, direct sales)
- Don't try to make BOTH easy — you'll end up with low value + low margins

### 7 Essential Elements

Every complete offer must contain all seven:

| # | Element | Question It Answers |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Outcome** | "What will I achieve?" |
| 2 | **Timeframe** | "How long will it take?" |
| 3 | **Method** | "How does it work?" |
| 4 | **Secrets** | "What do I get that I can't get elsewhere?" |
| 5 | **Safety Net** | "What if it doesn't work?" |
| 6 | **Polarization** | "Why should I choose YOU?" |
| 7 | **Pricing** | "What does it cost and is it worth it?" |

Missing any element creates a gap in the prospect's mental model and gives them a reason to hesitate.

## 3. Problem & Pain Mapping

### Before / During / After Problems

**Problems BEFORE they start:**
- What stops them from even beginning?
- Overwhelm, confusion, fear, not knowing where to start
- Pull from avatar's Roadblocks and Before State

**Problems DURING the process:**
- What makes them want to quit?
- Frustration, slow results, unexpected obstacles, lack of support
- Pull from avatar's Pain Points and emotional triggers

**Problems AFTER they achieve it:**
- What new problems emerge from success?
- Maintaining results, new identity challenges, scaling issues

For EACH problem, also consider:
- **Vehicle** (the "how" — what method are they using?)
- **Internal beliefs** (do they think THEY can do it?)
- **External beliefs** (do they trust YOUR method?)

### 5 Pools of Pain

Categorize every problem into these pools:

1. **Vehicle Pain** — Pain caused by the METHOD they're using (or lack of one). "I've tried X and it doesn't work." "I don't know where to start."
2. **Internal Pain** — Pain caused by their own BELIEFS. "I'm not tech-savvy enough." "I'm not the kind of person who can do marketing."
3. **External Pain** — Pain caused by CIRCUMSTANCES outside their control. "My competitors have bigger budgets." "The market is saturated."
4. **Philosophical Pain** — Pain caused by UNFAIRNESS or value misalignment. "It shouldn't be this hard to get clients." "I shouldn't have to become a marketer."
5. **Social Pain** — Pain caused by how others PERCEIVE them. "My family thinks I should get a real job." "Others seem to have it figured out."

### The Gap

The psychological distance between where your prospect IS and where they WANT TO BE.

```
Current Situation → [THE GAP] → Desired Situation
```

**Mapping The Gap:**
1. **Current situation** — Describe their reality in painful detail (use their words from avatar research)
2. **Desired situation** — Describe their dream state with visceral specificity
3. **The bridge** — Your offer IS the bridge across The Gap
4. **Gap size matters** — Too small = "I don't need that." Too large = "That's impossible for me."
5. **Goldilocks zone** — The gap should feel significant but achievable with your help

The bigger they FEEL the gap, the more they'll pay to close it. Your marketing's job is to make the gap VIVID — not to create it, but to illuminate it.

### Latent Conditions

Hidden psychological states that affect buying decisions but are rarely addressed directly:

- **Identity conflict** — "I'm not the kind of person who invests in coaching"
- **Authority skepticism** — "Online gurus are all scammers"
- **Analysis paralysis** — "I need to research more before deciding"
- **Timing delusion** — "I'll do it when things calm down"
- **Comparison trap** — "Maybe there's something better out there"
- **Impostor syndrome** — "I'm not ready / my business isn't big enough"

**How to Address:**
- Name them directly in your copy (this feels like mind-reading)
- Normalize them: "You might be thinking [condition] — that's completely normal"
- Reframe them: "The fact that you're worried about X means you're the right person"
- Use testimonials from people who had the same condition and overcame it

## 4. Confidence Engineering

### 3 Pools of Confidence

Prospects need confidence in THREE areas before buying. All 3 must be filled — filling 2 out of 3 still results in a non-buyer.

**Pool 1: Confidence in the VEHICLE**
- "Does this METHOD actually work?"
- Address with: case studies, data, logic, process transparency
- Show the method, explain WHY it works, prove it's worked before

**Pool 2: Confidence in YOU**
- "Can THIS person/company actually deliver?"
- Address with: credentials, testimonials, track record, authority markers
- Personal story, client results, industry recognition

**Pool 3: Confidence in THEMSELVES**
- "Can I actually do this / make this work for MY situation?"
- Address with: stories of similar people succeeding, ease of implementation, ongoing support
- This is the MOST overlooked pool — most offers ignore it
- "But my situation is different..." — address this directly

### The Action Threshold

The invisible line a prospect must cross before they'll buy.

**What Raises the Threshold (harder to buy):**
- Confusion about what they get
- Skepticism about whether it works
- Fear of wasting money/time
- Past failures with similar offers
- Perceived effort required
- Social risk ("what will people think?")

**What Lowers the Threshold (easier to buy):**
- Crystal clear offer communication
- Proof (testimonials, case studies, data)
- Risk reversal (guarantees)
- Social proof (others like them have done it)
- Low first step (micro-commitment)
- Urgency (cost of inaction)
- Authority (why YOU specifically)

**The Formula:**
```
Desire for Outcome + Pain of Current Situation > Action Threshold
```

Maximize the left side (through vivid copy) and minimize the right side (through offer design).

### 6 Pillars of an Irresistible Offer

Every offer must address all 6. Miss one and conversion drops.

1. **The Outcome** — What SPECIFIC result will they achieve? Must be viscerally desirable. Express in their language with sensory details.
2. **The Timeframe** — HOW LONG until results? Shorter = more valuable. Set interim milestones. Break long timeframes into phases.
3. **The Method** — HOW will they achieve it? Must be believable AND different. Name your method — proprietary naming increases perceived value.
4. **The Secrets** — What do they get that they CAN'T get elsewhere? Proprietary frameworks, insider knowledge, unique data. This is your competitive moat.
5. **The Safety Net** — What happens if it DOESN'T work? Guarantees, fallbacks, ongoing support. Signals confidence in your method.
6. **The Polarization** — Why choose YOU? Take a stance. Repel the wrong people to attract the right ones. Creates tribal loyalty.

## 5. Enhancement & Packaging

### Scarcity — Limited Supply
- **Physical:** Limited seats, spots, inventory
- **Cohort:** Only accepting X people per round
- **Rolling:** When someone leaves, a spot opens
- Must be GENUINE — manufactured scarcity destroys trust

### Urgency — Time Pressure
- **Cohort-based:** Doors close on [date]
- **Rolling deadline:** Price increases after X days
- **Seasonal:** Available only during [period]
- **Disappearing bonuses:** Bonus removed after deadline

### Bonuses — Added Value
- Each bonus should solve a SPECIFIC problem or objection
- Name them individually with their own perceived value
- Give each a dollar value
- Best bonuses: things that cost you little but save them a lot
- "Fast action" bonuses create urgency + reward decisive action

### Guarantees — Risk Reversal

**Types by approach:**

| Type | What You Promise | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Unconditional** | Full refund, no questions | Highest (you absorb all risk) |
| **Conditional** | Refund IF they do X, Y, Z | Medium (shared risk) |
| **Anti-guarantee** | "All sales final" — only with strong proof | Low (they absorb risk) |
| **Implied** | "Until you get [result]" — open-ended delivery | Medium |
| **Performance** | We hit [metric] or you don't pay | Highest |
| **Service** | We do [specific actions] guaranteed | Medium |

**Types by focus:**

| Type | Best For | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Results guarantee** | When you can control the outcome | "30 new members or we work free until you do" |
| **Service guarantee** | When results depend on client effort | "We'll publish 12 posts/month or your money back" |
| **Satisfaction guarantee** | When building trust with skeptical buyers | "Try it for 30 days — love it or full refund" |

**Guarantee stacking:** Combine types for maximum risk reversal. Lead with results, back with service, safety net with satisfaction.

### M.A.G.I.C. Naming Formula

| Letter | Element | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **M** | Make a Magnetic Reason Why | Why does this exist? Why now? |
| **A** | Announce Your Avatar | Who is this specifically for? |
| **G** | Give Them a Goal | What will they achieve? |
| **I** | Indicate a Time Interval | How quickly? |
| **C** | Complete with a Container Word | What IS it? |

**Container words:** System, Blueprint, Challenge, Bootcamp, Accelerator, Academy, Vault, Method, Formula, Protocol, Playbook, Sprint, Masterclass, Intensive

**Examples:**
- "The 6-Week Fit-Over-40 Challenge" (M=challenge urgency, A=over-40, G=fit, I=6 weeks, C=challenge)
- "Gym Owner's 90-Day Revenue Accelerator" (M=accelerate, A=gym owners, G=revenue, I=90 days, C=accelerator)

Score each name: which M.A.G.I.C. elements does it hit? Aim for 4+.

### The CUB Test

Run every offer through this filter before publishing:

| Test | Question | What to Fix |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **C — Confusing?** | Can a 10-year-old understand it? Is it clear what they get, do, and what happens? | Remove jargon, buzzwords, vague language. One offer, one audience, one outcome. |
| **U — Unbelievable?** | Does any claim sound too good to be true without proof? | Add testimonials, case studies, specific numbers, process transparency. Reduce claims or add conditions. |
| **B — Boring?** | Does it sound like every other offer? Does it create emotional response? | Add specific details, personality, vivid language, pattern interrupts. |

If ANY test fails, fix it before publishing.

## 6. Funnel & Conversion

### Micro-Step Funnel Flow

**Principle:** Every step is the "next logical yes." People don't go from stranger to customer in one step.

1. **Attention** — Pattern interrupt that stops the scroll
2. **Interest** — Relevant problem/solution that earns a click
3. **Micro-commitment** — Low-risk action (quiz, download, watch)
4. **Engagement** — Deeper interaction (read, consume, reply)
5. **Conversion** — The offer (buy, book, join)
6. **Activation** — First value delivered (onboarding, quick win)

**Rules:**
- Each step must deliver value ON ITS OWN (not just tease the next step)
- The gap between steps should feel tiny ("Of course I'll do that")
- Never ask for a commitment larger than the trust you've built
- Bonuses and ethical bribes grease the transitions
- If people drop off at a step, the gap is too big — add an intermediate step

### Two-Step Sale Structure

- **Step 1:** Small discount offer ($19-$99) to get card on file and create initial transaction
- **Step 2:** Prospect shows up, gets value, then upsold to core offer
- **Benefits:** 85-90% show rates (vs 50-60% for free), card already on file, "foot in the door" principle

### Premium vs Free vs Discount Decision Tree

| Type | Best When | Close Rate | CAC | Key Advantage |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----|---------------|
| **Premium** | Proven offer, strong sales, cash to invest | Lower volume, higher ticket | Highest | Simplest math, best customers |
| **Free** | Starting out, testing demand, entering cold markets | Same as premium | 5-10x lower | Highest volume, fastest flow |
| **Discount** | Regulated industries, two-step sale, card on file | Same as premium | Lower | Legal compliance, card capture |

**Decision Sequence:**
```
Starting out / low volume → Free or Discount wrapper
Established with conviction + cash → Premium standalone
Order: Generate Flow → Monetize Flow → Add Friction
```

### Benefit Bullets Format

```
**[Bold Headline — the benefit in 5-8 words]**
[One sentence explaining HOW they get this benefit and WHY it matters]
```

**Rules:**
- 5-7 bullets maximum (more dilutes impact)
- Each addresses a different concern or desire
- Alternate emotional and logical benefits
- Lead with strongest, end with second strongest
- Every bullet answers: "What does this mean for ME?"

### The Pitch (100-200 Words)

A complete offer summary that works in ANY context — landing page, email, DM, conversation.

1. **Problem acknowledgment** (1-2 sentences) — Show you understand their pain
2. **Dream outcome** (1 sentence) — Paint the picture of what's possible
3. **How it works** (2-3 sentences) — Your method in plain language
4. **What they get** (2-3 sentences) — Tangible deliverables
5. **Guarantee / risk reversal** (1 sentence) — Remove the risk
6. **CTA** (1 sentence) — The next logical step

**Test:** Read it out loud. If it sounds like a conversation, it's right. If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it.

### Commodity Problem — Speed / Risk / Ease

When your offer sounds like everyone else's, differentiate on three vectors:

| Vector | Core Question | Example |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Speed** | "What would it take to get this in 1/3 the time?" | Pay contractors 50% more for same-day/next-day |
| **Risk** | "Can we insure or guarantee against the biggest fear?" | Insurance policy on their fear, included in price |
| **Ease** | "How much can we prepare the way ahead of time?" | Pre-work, intake prep, seamless onboarding |

- Pick 1-2 vectors and go all-in
- Raise prices to reflect the premium — you are no longer commoditized
- If current market won't pay, go upmarket
