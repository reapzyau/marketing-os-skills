# LinkedIn Guidelines

Platform-specific formatting, algorithm signals, and best practices for LinkedIn posts.

---

## Character Limits

- **Maximum:** 3,000 characters per post
- **Optimal:** 500-1,500 characters (medium-length sweet spot — see research note below)
- **Hook limit:** 125 characters before the "see more" fold
- **First line is everything.** If they don't click "see more," nothing else matters.

**Research note (March 2026):** Analysis of 192 high-engagement posts found medium-length posts (500-1,500 chars) averaged 71% higher engagement than short posts (<500 chars). Long posts (>1,500 chars) scored even higher per-post but had very low volume — medium is the practical sweet spot. Enough depth to prove expertise, short enough to hold attention.

---

## Formatting Rules

1. **Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences.** Dense paragraphs kill engagement.
2. **Short sentences.** Vary length — short punches, medium carries, long builds.
3. **No links in body.** LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with outbound links. Put links in first comment.
4. **Hashtags in comments**, not in the post body. If using hashtags at all, 3-5 max in first comment.
5. **No markdown rendering.** LinkedIn doesn't render bold/italic markdown. Use CAPS or spacing for emphasis.
6. **Emojis:** Sparingly. Never in the hook line. 2-3 max per post. Use as bullet replacements if needed.
7. **Lists:** Use numbers (1. 2. 3.) or dashes (- item). Both render cleanly.
8. **Charts and data images boost engagement.** Image posts (charts, screenshots, data visualisations) averaged ~2x the engagement of text-only posts in a March 2026 analysis of 192 high-engagement posts. Stock photos and generic graphics do NOT help — the image must add informational value. If your post references data or a visual concept, always include a chart or screenshot.

## Algorithm Signals (What LinkedIn Rewards)

**Ranked by importance:**

1. **Dwell time** — How long people spend reading your post. Longer posts that hold attention win.
2. **Comments** — Especially in the first 60-90 minutes. Reply to every comment quickly.
3. **Saves** — The strongest signal of value. Educational, actionable, reference-worthy content drives saves.
4. **Shares** — Reposting to someone's network. Controversial and insightful content gets shared.
5. **Reactions** — Likes matter least but still count. "Insightful" reactions may weight more than "Like."

**What LinkedIn suppresses:**
- External links in post body
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree!")
- Editing posts within the first hour
- Tagging people who don't engage back
- Posting more than once per day (diminishing returns)

---

## Posting Strategy

- **Frequency:** 1 post per day, 5 days per week. Quality over quantity.
- **Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am or 11am-12pm (user's timezone). Test and adjust.
- **Worst times:** Weekends and late evenings (lower engagement baseline).
- **Reply window:** First 90 minutes are critical. Reply to every comment to boost the post.
- **First comment:** Post your CTA, link, or hashtags as the first comment immediately after publishing.

---

## Content That Performs on LinkedIn

**High performers (ranked by engagement data, March 2026):**
- "I analysed X" data authority posts with charts (highest — ~2x avg engagement)
- Provocative questions that challenge assumptions
- Contrarian reframes ("We think X. Actually Y.")
- Frameworks and systems (saveable)
- Personal stories with business lessons
- Lists of tools/resources/tips

**Low performers:**
- Generic motivational quotes
- Company announcements without story or lesson
- Link-heavy posts
- Pure self-promotion without value
- Walls of text with no formatting
- Short text-only posts with no depth or data

---

## LinkedIn-Specific Adaptation Rules

When adapting content for LinkedIn:
- **Expand:** LinkedIn allows more space. Add context, stories, and explanations.
- **Platform language:** Say "LinkedIn" not "social media" when referring to the platform.
- **Professional framing:** Same idea, more business context. "Founders" not "people."
- **CTA style:** "Drop your best tip below" or "Which one resonates?" — LinkedIn audiences engage with direct questions.
- **AMA format:** Works well. "Ask me anything about [topic] for the next 60 minutes. Drop your questions below."

## Post Anatomy (Optimal Structure)

```
[HOOK — under 125 chars, curiosity or recognition]
                                                    <- "see more" fold
[SETUP — 1-2 sentences, context for why this matters]

[BODY — the value, formatted with line breaks and lists]

[TAKEAWAY — one clear sentence]

[CTA — specific engagement prompt]
```

**First comment (posted immediately):**
```
[Link if relevant]
[3-5 hashtags if using]
[Or: additional context / resource mentioned in post]
```
