# TVML Money Model Framework Reference

This framework is inspired by Alex Hormozi's work on offer economics and business model design.

---

## 1. Economics & Payback

### The 30-Day Payback Principle

A deliberate sequence of offers designed to make enough profit from one customer to get and service at least two more customers in less than 30 days.

**The 3 Levers:**
1. Make customers **more valuable** (they pay more)
2. Get **more of them** (volume)
3. Get paid **faster** (velocity)

Double all three = **8x faster** growth. Triple = **27x**.

Any business can get interest-free money for 30 days via credit card. If you clear your balance before month-end, it's free capital.

1. Use credit to acquire a customer
2. Make enough profit from that customer within 30 days to pay it back
3. Use that money to acquire the next customer
4. Rinse and repeat

**Two tiers:**
- **Good Money Model**: 30-day GP from one customer > CAC + cost to service
- **Elite Money Model**: 30-day GP from ONE customer > cost to get and service MANY customers

### CFA (Customer Financed Acquisition)

**Core Formula:**
```
30-Day Profit Per Customer > Cost to Acquire + Cost to Service
```

**CAC Calculation:**
```
CAC = (Advertising + Sales Compensation + Software + Commissions) / Customers Acquired
```

| Channel | Example Calculation | CAC |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| Outreach | ($3K emailer + $200 software + $800 commissions) / 8 customers | $500 |
| Content | ($10K media payroll + $1K commissions) / 10 customers | $1,100 |
| Paid Ads | ($4K media buyer + $20K ad spend + $1K software + $10K commissions) / 10 customers | $3,500 |

**LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit):**
- **Step 1:** Gross Profit per customer = Price - Cost of Fulfillment
- **Step 2a (Products):** Average # of lifetime transactions
- **Step 2b (Recurring):** Churn % = customers lost / customers at period start
- **Step 3a (Products):** LTGP = GP x Avg Transactions (e.g., $80 x 4 = $360)
- **Step 3b (Recurring):** LTGP = GP / Churn % (e.g., $2,400 / 5% = $48,000)

**Payback Period (PPD):**
```
PPD = Time until cumulative GP > CAC
```
Target: 30D Cash > 2x CAC minimum.

### Three Levels of CFA

| Level | Condition | Result |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | 30D GP < CAC | Must float on savings/loans. Risky. |
| 2 | 30D GP = CAC | Credit limit = ad budget. Capped but sustainable. |
| 3 | 30D GP > 2x CAC | Every customer funds the next. Eliminates cash as bottleneck. |

Level 3 played out: $1 invested → 4,095 customers in 12 months (doubling monthly).

### CFA 3-Lever Expanded

| Lever | Focus | Playbooks |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **1: Lower CAC** | Spend less to acquire | Ads, hooks, branding, marketing machine |
| **2: Increase LTGP** | Make more per customer | Delivery, upsell timing, ascension |
| **3: Accelerate Cashflow (decrease PPD)** | Get paid faster | Pricing, cashflow tactics, prepayment |

**Upsell Timing Windows (Critical):**
1. Immediately at point of sale
2. 24-48 hours after (they realize they need something)
3. At milestone points
4. Halfway through
5. Last chance (end of program)

Waiting until end to upsell = lose 2-3x potential sales.

### 6M Constraint Framework

A linear diagnostic decision tree for identifying your single business constraint. Exit at the first and earliest ramp.

**Sequence: More → Metrics → Market → Model → Money → Manpower**

**M1 — MORE:** "Why can't we do more of what's already working?" More is the highest risk-adjusted return move. If something worked, do more. Diminishing returns are still returns. Count in hundreds.

**M2 — METRICS:** "We can't do more because we don't know our numbers." Track: Leads, Scheduled, Shows, Sales, CAC, LTGP, Revenue, Profit, Cashflow. Speed of business = speed of decision-making.

**M3 — MARKET:** "My market is too small." Almost never true unless local with <500 people. Most people use one method, one platform, one medium — a tiny slice of a bigger pie.

**M4 — MODEL:** "I'm not sure if this business is worth it." Usually a mindset/timeline issue. "If this business made the money you wanted, would you still want to change?" Most opportunities can reach massive scale with enough time.

**M5 — MONEY (4 Sub-Constraints):**

| Sub-Constraint | Solution |
|---------------|----------|
| Leads cost too much | Better advertising |
| Close too few | Better sales / lead nurture |
| Don't make enough per customer | Increase LTGP (raise price, decrease costs, increase frequency, ascend/upsell) |
| Make enough but too slowly | Cash flow problem → 9 cashflow tactics |

**M6 — MANPOWER:** "Can't handle more volume." Supply constraint = demand constraint for employees. Same acquisition framework applies to recruiting talent.

## 2. Offer Sequencing

### The 3-Stage Build Sequence

Build one stage at a time. Never implement all at once.

| Stage | Focus | What It Does |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **I: Get Cash** | Attraction Offers | Get more customers for less |
| **II: Get More Cash** | Upsell + Downsell Offers | Make more money from them faster |
| **III: Get The Most Cash** | Continuity Offers | Maximize total money spent |

Each stage must pay for the next. Measure in quarters, not weeks.

### All 16 Offer Mechanisms

**ATTRACTION OFFERS (5) — Turn strangers into customers**

1. **Win Your Money Back** — Customer pays upfront. If they meet criteria (results/actions), they get money back as cash or store credit. ~10% ask for money back. Spread store credit over longer periods ($600 over 12 months = $50/mo discount). Make everyone a winner privately.

2. **Giveaways** — Advertise a chance to win a grand prize in exchange for contact info. Everyone else gets a "promotional offer" (10-30% discount off gross margins). Steps: Pick grand prize, pick promotional offer, collect contact info + eligibility criteria, set deadline (3-7 days), announce winner, contact everyone else. Give two prizes for 2x leads.

3. **Decoy Offer** — Advertise a free/discounted version (the decoy). When leads engage, present a far more valuable premium offer side-by-side. Make the contrast huge — strip the decoy, load the premium. Ask "Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?" ~80% take the premium when done right.

4. **Buy X Get Y Free** — When customers buy something, they get other stuff free. Give MORE free than paid (buy 1 get 2 > buy 2 get 1). Raise prices to accommodate. Can reframe existing pricing.

5. **Pay Less Now or Pay More Later** — Give people a choice: pay full price later (with guarantee) OR pay a discounted price now (with bonuses). Get card on file with the "pay later" option.

**UPSELL OFFERS (4) — Get people to spend more**

6. **Classic Upsell** — Offer the solution to the customer's next problem the moment they become aware of it. "You can't have X without Y." Every offer solves one problem and creates another.

7. **Menu Upsell** — Four tactics in sequence: (1) Unselling — tell them what they DON'T need, cross items out, builds trust. (2) Prescription Upsell — tell them what they DO need with personalized instructions, assumed close. (3) A/B Upsell — ask preference between two options, never yes/no. (4) Card On File — "Wanna just use the card on file?" Reduces friction.

8. **Anchor Upsell** — Present the most expensive option first (5-10x the main offer price). Get "The Gasp." Come to the rescue by asking if they care about premium features. Present main offer — it looks like a steal. Some actually buy the premium.

9. **Rollover Upsell** — Credit some or all of a customer's previous purchases toward the next offer. Instead of giving $600 store credit as 3 free months, spread it as $50/mo discount over 12 months.

**DOWNSELL OFFERS (3) — Turn nos into yeses**

10. **Payment Plan Downsell** — Same product, same price, different payment structure. "Seesaw" method: Full price → Half now, half later → Three payments → "What can you do?" → Charge on their payday.

11. **Trial With Penalty** — Let customers try free IF they meet specific terms. If they don't meet terms, they pay a penalty fee. Terms should activate and retain. Always get card upfront.

12. **Feature Downsell** — Lower the price by changing what they get. Remove quantity, quality, guarantee, or optional components. Temperature check after 2 downsells: "On a scale 1-10 how bad do you want this?" 8+ = keep going. 7 or below = "What would a 10 look like?" and recombine features.

**CONTINUITY OFFERS (4) — Keep people buying**

13. **Continuity Bonus** — Give an awesome bonus if they sign up for continuity today. Bonus value > first continuity payment. Stack bonuses and mention individual dollar values.

14. **Continuity Discount (Lifetime Discount)** — Give free time (now or later) if they commit today. "Lifetime Discount" variant: lock in a lower rate forever if they commit now.

15. **Waived Fee Offer** — Two options: Month-to-month (pay setup fee 3-5x monthly rate + monthly rate, cancel anytime) vs. Commitment (12+ months, fee waived, fee applies if cancel early). Customers stay because leaving costs more than staying.

16. **Automatic Renewal** — Turn any Attraction Offer into Continuity by adding automatic renewal at the end.

### Mechanism Scorecard Format

Score each mechanism 1-10 against the core offer on three dimensions:

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Fit | How naturally does this mechanism match the core offer and business model? |
| Effort | How much work to implement? (10 = easiest) |
| Impact | Expected revenue or conversion impact? |

Select 1-2 best fits per category (Attraction, Upsell, Downsell, Continuity).

### Premium vs Free vs Discount Decision Tree

**Premium Offers:**
- Simplest math, fewest moving parts. Only quality customers. Highest ticket.
- Most expensive CPL. Requires strong sales skills and deep avatar knowledge.
- Math: 5% of prospects buy at $10,000 ($9,500 profit) = 9.5x more profitable than 100% buying at $100 ($50 profit)
- Best when: Proven offer, strong sales process, cash to burn learning

**Free Offers:**
- Highest lead volume. Lowest lead cost. Fastest way to test demand.
- Can overwhelm operations. Some people never intend to buy.
- "Penny gap": 9x more people take free vs. 1 cent
- Split test data: Close rate identical between free and non-free respondents. Lead costs 5x+ lower for free.

**Discount Offers (50%+ only):**
- Legal compliance in regulated industries. Card on file makes upsells seamless. Two-step sale.
- Can train customers to only buy on discount. Attracts bargain hoppers.
- Best when: Well-understood services where people know the price range

**Decision Sequence:**
```
Starting out / low volume → Free or Discount wrapper
Established with conviction + cash → Premium standalone
Order: Generate Flow → Monetize Flow → Add Friction
```

### Two-Step Sale Structure

1. **Step 1:** Sell a small discount offer ($19-$99) to get card on file
2. **Step 2:** Prospect shows up, gets value, then is upsold to the core offer

Benefits: Eliminates "forgot my card" objection, show rates 85-90%+ (vs. 50-60% for free), "foot in the door" principle, frictionless upsell close.

### Advanced Offer Stacking (Value Grid)

Instead of a linear value ladder, use a **grid** to visualize all offers x all customers. Customers don't buy linearly — they skip, buy out of sequence.

Example: Basic grid = $75 30D Cash vs. stacked grid = $1,763 30D Cash (14-15x difference).

**Four Steps to Picking the Right Offer:**
1. **Right Stage:** Match offer to Money Model stage (attraction, upsell, continuity)
2. **Right Problem:** Pick problems you can solve with existing resources that provide big value
3. **Right Way:** Solve based on customer's preference, not yours
4. **Right Time:** Offer at the moment of greatest need, not when convenient for you

## 3. Pricing Architecture

### 80/20 Pareto Fractal

Customers follow Pareto's 80/20 distribution. Price by willingness to pay, not cost-plus.

```
Base:    100 people x $100    = $10,000
5x tier:  20 people x $500    = $10,000
25x tier:  4 people x $2,500  = $10,000
                                --------
Total:   124 people            = $30,000
```

- Each upsell tier should be ~5x the previous price
- Expect ~20% uptake per tier
- Each tier roughly doubles total revenue
- Customer count only increases 24% for 200% more revenue

### Anchor Pricing

Present the 5-10x premium option first. The "real" offer looks like a bargain by comparison.

### Decoy Pricing

To sell the premium option, add a decoy at the same price:
- A: Digital — $59/yr
- B: Print — $125/yr
- C: Digital + Print — $125/yr → Everyone picks C.

### Small-Medium-Large Nudging

- Push to Large: price Medium irrationally high (S:$5, M:$8, L:$9)
- Push to Medium: price Small irrationally high (S:$6, M:$7, L:$9)
- If most buy Large already: raise all prices

### Continuity vs. Upfront Pricing Ratios

| Standalone Multiplier | % Choose Continuity |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| 1.33x | 50% |
| 1.66x | 60% |
| 2x | 70% |
| 2.33x | 80% |
| 2.66x | 90% |

Example: $199/mo continuity. For 70% choosing continuity, price standalone at ~$399 (2x monthly).

### Prepayment Structures

| Type | How It Works | When to Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Guarantee + Prepay** | "I'll give you the guarantee if you prepay. No prepay, no guarantee." | High-ticket services where guarantees are a differentiator |
| **Layaway** | "Start paying now. When you've paid half/full, we start working." | Reverses leverage — you get paid before you deliver |
| **BNPL** | Third-party financing (Affirm, Klarna, etc.) | Typically ~35% increase in sales |

### Quarterly Pricing Structure

For recurring businesses with cashflow problems:
- Primary offer = quarterly (not month-to-month)
- Annual gets a discount
- Month-to-month is more expensive
- Single shift accelerates cashflow by 3x

### Weekly vs. Monthly Billing

Weekly billing advantages:
- Smaller amounts = easier for customers to pay
- Failed payments caught faster
- Faster cash collection velocity
- More touchpoints for relationship
- Collects more reliably overall

## 4. Cashflow Acceleration

### 9 Tactics

| # | Tactic | Impact |
|---|--------|--------|
| 1 | Push out vendor terms (Net 30 → Net 60/90) | Accelerates cash by 30-90 days |
| 2 | Quarterly as primary pricing | 3x faster collection |

| 9 | AR financing (accounts receivable financing through banks) | For established businesses |

## 5. Acquisition Engine

### Commodity Problem Differentiation (Speed / Risk / Ease)

When your offer is easily compared to competitors, win on at least one of three vectors:

| Vector | Core Question | Example |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Speed** | "What would it take to get this in 1/3 the time?" | Pay contractors 50% more for same-day/next-day |
| **Risk** | "Can we insure or guarantee against the biggest fear?" | Insurance policy on their biggest fear, included in price |
| **Ease** | "How much can we prepare the way ahead of time?" | Pre-work, intake prep, onboarding so experience is seamless |

Pick 1-2 vectors and go all-in. Raise prices to reflect the premium. If your current market won't pay, go upmarket.

### Lead Magnet vs. Direct Offer Tradeoff

| Dimension | Direct Offer | Lead Magnet |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Short-term ROAS | Higher | Lower |
| Long-term ROAS | Lower | Higher |
| Scalability | Limited (warmest prospects only) | Much greater (broader awareness) |
| Audience reached | Most Aware + Product Aware | All 5 awareness levels |

**Rule:** Small money → direct offers. Massive scale → lead magnets with conversion process.

### Awareness Ladder (5 Stages)

| Level | Description | Hook Type | Example |
|-------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Most Aware** | Knows your product, needs the deal | Offer-driven | "First month free" |
| **Product Aware** | Knows you, not sure you're right | Proof-driven | "Why 9/10 users reach their goal" |
| **Solution Aware** | Knows the result, doesn't know your product | Promise-driven | "Lose 15lbs in 30 days" |
| **Problem Aware** | Senses a problem, doesn't know solutions exist | Pain-driven | "Frustrated with crash diets?" |
| **Unaware** | Doesn't know they have a problem | Curiosity-driven | "The hidden reason 1 in 3 gain weight" |

### Content Scaling Phases

**Phase 1: Barely Alive** — Businesses doing outbound or ads as primary channel. Goal: Show you have a pulse. Volume: 1-3 posts/week. People check your profile and think "okay, they're legit."

**Phase 2: Momentum** — Leads start coming from content; they close fast, pay more, are warmer. Start posting as much as humanly possible.

**Phase 3: Acceleration (Machine Building)** — Build media infrastructure. Paid and organic merge into "media." Content that works organically gets CTAs spliced in and runs as paid. Brand + direct return simultaneously.

### BAMFAM Flow (Book A Meeting From A Meeting)

Every appointment must end by scheduling the next appointment. Don't let a customer leave without booking the next interaction. The more times you can upsell, the more people you will upsell. BAMFAM creates those opportunities.

### 7 Ad Creation Methods

1. **Life Cycle Documentation** — Document customer journey (before/during/after). Every stage = raw footage for ads.
2. **In-Person Events & Appreciation** — Host events; everyone happily records content/testimonials.
3. **Community, Review Site & Communication Scrapes** — Pull reviews from review platforms. Screenshot them. Each review = potential ad.
4. **Testimonial Competition** — Grand prize to customer base. Entry = submit a video ad following your best ad as a script.
5. **Delivery or Outcome Moments** — Capture unboxings, transformations, milestones. Tie testimonials to milestone recognition.
6. **Organic + CTA Splice** — Take best-performing organic content, add CTA at end, run as paid ad.
7. **UGC / Influencer Army** — Build zealots who make content for you. Whitelist influencer accounts to run ads from their profiles.

**Kaleidoscope Method (Scaling Winners):** Once you find a winning ad, make 100+ permutations by changing: color, font, actor, clothing, background, speed, spokesperson. Messaging stays the same. Can 5-10x ad set output.

### Platform CTA Frameworks

**YouTube:** Profile bio link, below-the-fold description, 1/3 mark of video, product button, community posts.

**Instagram:** Single CTA on profile bio, chat integration auto-DMs, pinned posts, story CTAs, trial reels (up to 5 hook permutations/day).

**TikTok:** Bio link, comment pinning, TikTok Shop for e-commerce.

**LinkedIn:** Profile CTA, post CTAs ("Comment WORD for my free [thing]"). Best for B2B.

**Email:** Subject line = brand not tricks. Reward the open. Get to value ASAP. CTA. PS = reward cycle. Email can drive 25-50% of revenue.

### Brand as 401k

Brand is a compounding investment, not a one-time expense. Brand lifts everything: outreach, paid ads, organic. Brand = pricing power + demand.

**How brand works (aspirational achievement model):** Achieve things orders of magnitude greater than your avatar's aspirations. Document it publicly so they reverse-engineer the believability.

## 6. Simplification Framework

Applied to every process in business — a 5-step optimization review:

| Step | Action | Key Question |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Question** | Why are we doing this at all? | "If there's no point, don't do it." |
| **2. Delete** | Remove everything unnecessary | Eliminate steps, tools, processes that don't contribute |
| **3. Optimize** | Find points of greatest leverage | What gives the most output per input? |
| **4. Accelerate** | Compress the timeline | "Why can't you get it done today?" |
| **5. Automate** | Remove human involvement | Only after the other 4 steps are complete |

### More-Better-New Framework

| Strategy | When | Risk |
|----------|------|------|
| **More** | Almost always first (especially <$1M/year) | Lowest — what you're doing already works |
| **Better** | When incremental return on "better" > return on "more" | Medium |
| **New** | Only when you've maximized a market or platform is shrinking | Highest |

**The Accordion Effect:** More and Better alternate. Scale volume → notice one winner → focus on quality → scale volume at new quality level → repeat.

### The 20% Cost of Change Rule

Every change carries a **guaranteed ~20% decrease in performance** with only a variable upside.
- If potential gain < 20%, don't make the change
- Serial changes compound the problem
- If you change nothing, business naturally improves ~3%/year

### Single Input, Multiple Outputs

Create leverage by extracting multiple outputs from one activity. Always ask "what's the sawdust?" — what other outputs can be extracted from existing behavior?

## Key Principles

1. **Simple scales, fancy fails** — 100 ways to offer your product > 100 products
2. **Every solution creates a new problem** — every new problem is an upsell opportunity
3. **Hard selling is for weak products** — make offers available at the time customers have a problem
4. **Raise price in stages** — start cheap for yeses and feedback, keep raising until total revenue drops
5. **Generate flow → monetize flow → add friction** — in that order, always
6. **Quality > Quantity in customer selection** — every time qualification steps were removed, money went down
7. **Sell to people who don't stop buying** — fortunes are created when you sell things customers never stop buying
8. **Count in thousands, not tens** — volume creates leverage
9. **Constraint is the priority** — there is only ONE constraint at any time. Find it. Everything else is potential, not throughput.
10. **The 4 ways to frame the same discount** — percentage off, dollar amount, free portion, total package (test which converts best). Rule of thumb: percentage for under $100, dollar amount for over $100.
